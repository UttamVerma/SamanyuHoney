Beeswax is a natural product and commercially useful wax secreted from the underside glands of the bee abdomen; bees mould the wax to form honeycomb. It is a substance produced by bees to build honeycombs. It is collected by heating the honeycomb in water (after removing the honey) so that the floating wax can be separated after solidification when cool.

Our experienced and highly-skilled team meticulously extract out wax from the emptied honeycombs. The process begins by melting the emptied honeycombs in boiling water at around 61-69 degree Celsius so that the pure liquid wax gets separated, and then is allowed to cool down gradually so that the wax solidifies. This extracted beeswax is used to make candles, polishes, inks, cosmetics and ointments.
